Ginger and its Benefits

25th August, 2020

Its Benefits

Ginger is a common folk treatment for upset stomach and nausea. There's evidence that it helps.
Ginger seems to aid digestion and saliva flow. Studies found that taking ginger could reduce nausea
and vomiting in some pregnant women. But pregnant women should be careful with ginger. Some experts
worry that it could raise the risk of miscarriage, especially in high doses. Ginger seems to help with
nausea caused by vertigo as well. There's mixed evidence about whether ginger helps with nausea caused
by motion sickness, surgery, or chemotherapy. Ginger does seem to help with painful periods. In one
study, more than 60% of women felt that ginger lessened pain.

Side Effects of Ginger

In small doses, ginger has few side effects. High doses of ginger, say, more than 5 grams a day,
increases the chances of side effects. Ginger on the skin may cause a rash. Consumption may cause:

  • Gas
  • Heartburn
  • Upset stomach
  • Mouth irritation

Love for Calories

28th August, 2020

Calories, we all know can be dangerous to our bodies if taken in excess, but them, how many of us know
that they make up the bulk of most of our favorite delicacies? From frries, to pastries, to sweets, iced
creams, fast food,s beaverages, and so on. As much as we campaign against taking in calories in excess,
how many of us are actually able to curb them out of our day to day meeal consumption?

Contrary to popular belief, some calories are good for the body and are actually required for the
body's proper functioning. On the other hand, others are bad, and can cause harm if taken in excess.

Effects of Low intake of Calories

Calories are critical when you're trying to lose weight, but low calorie diets eventually catch up to you.
If you're eating less than 1,200 calories a day, you might experience negative side-effects. Your body begins
to fight weight loss by lowering your metabolism and releasing hormones that make you hungry.

  • Risk of Nutrient deficiency
  • Reducing calories burns mass
  • Fewer calories boosts hunger

Effects of Taking too much Calories

Weight gain is a major effect of eating too many calories. Your body needs enough calories to function, but
when you take in more than your body uses, it stores the excess calories in the form of fat. Extra calories are
mainly stored in the form of triglycerides, which, when elevated, places your heart health at risk. As with other
lipids, too many triglycerides accumulate in your arteries, increasing the risk of your arteries becoming hard,
stiff and narrow -- a condition known as atherosclerosis. Stiffening of the artery wall increases the risk of having
a heart attack or stroke.

  • Can cause too much carbohydrate accumulation
  • Too much calories form saturated fat
  • Too much calories can lead to stroke
